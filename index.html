Social Engineering Awareness

Understanding the dangers of malicious links and QR codes

What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is a manipulation tactic that attackers use to trick individuals into revealing confidential information or performing specific actions.

How Hackers Use Links and QR Codes

One of the most common methods attackers use is through malicious links or QR codes. These methods involve tricking users into visiting a website or executing commands that can steal personal information or compromise devices.

Malicious Links

  • Hackers can send malicious links through emails, social media, or messages.
  • These links might appear legitimate, but they often redirect users to fake websites designed to steal login credentials or install malware.
  • Always double-check the linkâ€™s actual URL before clicking by hovering over it, especially in suspicious emails.

QR Codes

  • QR codes are increasingly used to direct users to websites, but they can easily be tampered with or replaced by malicious codes.
  • Hackers may use QR codes to execute commands on your device without your knowledge or take you to phishing websites.
  • Always verify the source of a QR code before scanning, especially in untrusted environments.

How to Protect Yourself

Awareness is the key to protecting yourself from these attacks. Here are some tips:

  • Always be cautious of links and QR codes sent from unknown sources.
  • Use trusted cybersecurity tools like antivirus and anti-phishing software to detect malicious websites.
  • Regularly update your devices to ensure vulnerabilities are patched.
  • If unsure about a link or QR code, use a URL checker or a trusted QR scanner before interacting.
